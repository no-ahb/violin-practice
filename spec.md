# Violin Practice Plan

45 min daily, 5x/week. Pre-show cycle. Bach runs parallel, for fun.

-----

## Time allocation

|Block                           |Time  |
|--------------------------------|------|
|Scales (intonation + modal work)|12 min|
|Adagio (polish)                 |6 min |
|Fuga (learn)                    |12 min|
|Improv                          |15 min|

-----

## The blocks in detail

### Scales (12 min)

Three things happening at once: intonation calibration, hand geography, theory fluency. Most scale practice only hits the middle one.

The block breaks into three sub-blocks, each running against a drone:

- **Technical scalework, 5 min** — scale, thirds, arpeggios, 7th chords
- **Modal work, 4 min** — same-tonic or same-key modal drills
- **Chord-scale fluency, 3 min** — scale-switching over progressions

Drone runs throughout. What you play against it changes.

-----

**Technical scalework, 5 min.**

The app drives this. It picks the key for the week, sets a drone, runs a metronome. You play.

*Key rotation — fifths, 2 weeks per tonic (minor then major):*

|Weeks|Tonic  |Minor (week 1)|Major (week 2)|
|-----|-------|--------------|--------------|
|1-2  |G      |G minor       |G major       |
|3-4  |D      |D minor       |D major       |
|5-6  |A      |A minor       |A major       |
|7-8  |E      |E minor       |E major       |
|9-10 |B      |B minor       |B major       |
|11-12|F# / Gb|F# minor      |Gb major      |

Twelve weeks through the first six tonics (the "easier" half of the circle). Then the next 12 weeks cover the flat side: C#/Db, G#/Ab, D#/Eb, Bb, F, C. Full cycle: 24 weeks. Each tonic comes around twice a year, with minor and major on consecutive weeks — you feel the parallel-major/minor relationship directly.

Alternatively: if a tonic feels unfinished after 2 weeks, the app lets you extend. Manual override.

*Minor scale forms within a minor week:*

- Mon / Wed / Fri: melodic ascending, natural descending (classical default — this is what the Bach uses and what most repertoire actually asks for)
- Tue: harmonic minor both directions (the scale that actually matches V7-i harmony, with the augmented 2nd between 6 and 7)
- Thu: melodic minor both directions (jazz usage — opens the door to the modes of melodic minor: lydian dominant, altered scale, etc.)

Major-key weeks: just the major scale both directions. No complication.

*What you play in the 5 min, in order:*

1. Scale, 3 octaves, ascending and descending. Quarter notes at the current tempo. (~1:15)
1. Scale in broken thirds: 1-3, 2-4, 3-5, 4-6, 5-7… up and back. 2 octaves (3 is too long here). (~1:15)
1. Tonic arpeggio, 3 octaves. (~45 sec)
1. Dominant 7th arpeggio, 2 octaves. (~45 sec)
1. Either subdominant arpeggio OR diminished 7th on the leading tone — alternate days. 2 octaves. (~45 sec)

All of this against the drone. Metronome at 60 to start. You bump it manually when you're ready — the app has a "bump tempo" button, it suggests a bump after 5 clean sessions at a given tempo but waits for your confirm.

*Drone pitch rotates daily through the week:*

- Mon: tonic (for G minor week, drone = G)
- Tue: **raised 7 on minor weeks (F# against G minor — matches harmonic minor, which is the day's scale form)**; 3rd on major weeks (B against G major)
- Wed: 5th (for G minor, drone = D)
- Thu: 7th (for G minor, drone = F; for G major, drone = F#)
- Fri: 6th — b6 for minor weeks (Eb against G minor, suspended aeolian context), natural 6 for major weeks (E against G major, relative minor felt from above)

Same scale all week, drone pitch shifts — the scale reorients harmonically against each reference.

*Bowing rotates daily, independent of drone and scale form:*

- Mon: sustained (one note per bow — reveals intonation drift clearly)
- Tue: detaché
- Wed: slurred 4
- Thu: slurred 8
- Fri: free / dealer's choice — note what you picked

The bowing changes what the intonation check reveals. Sustained bows show you every wobble. Slurred bows hide sharps under legato. Rotating forces you to confront each mode of hearing.

*Drone specifics.* Tanpura or shruti sample, looped, played from the browser via Web Audio. Not a sine wave — you need harmonic richness so intervals actually lock. Pre-rendered per tonic (12 samples per drone set) rather than real-time pitch-shifted, so temperament stays clean. Default tuning: just intonation — the beats lock more decisively than equal temperament, which is the point of drone practice. ET available as a setting. Reference pitch configurable (default A=440).

Practice instruction is simple: tune each note until the beats against the drone stop. Your ear does the rest.

**Modal work, 4 min.**

Modal work stays locked to the week's tonic. If the scales block is working G this week, modal is working G too — you're building a deep map of this tonic before moving on.

*Daily rotation across the 2-week tonic block:*

The schedule pairs same-tonic days with same-key days that drill the *same mode on a different tonic*. You hear each mode twice, from two angles, in consecutive days.

Week 1 (minor week on G — note collection is G A Bb C D Eb F):

|Day|Mode      |Approach                                             |
|---|----------|-----------------------------------------------------|
|Mon|G dorian  |same-tonic on G                                      |
|Tue|C dorian  |same-key (starts on 4th degree of G minor collection)|
|Wed|G phrygian|same-tonic on G                                      |
|Thu|D phrygian|same-key (starts on 5th degree of G minor collection)|
|Fri|G locrian |same-tonic on G                                      |

Across week 1 you drill dorian (on G and C), phrygian (on G and D), and locrian (on G). Natural minor on G is the implicit default from the scales block.

Week 2 (major week on G — note collection is G A B C D E F#):

|Day|Mode        |Approach                                             |
|---|------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
|Mon|G lydian    |same-tonic on G                                      |
|Tue|C lydian    |same-key (starts on 4th degree of G major collection)|
|Wed|G mixolydian|same-tonic on G                                      |
|Thu|D mixolydian|same-key (starts on 5th degree of G major collection)|
|Fri|G aeolian   |same-tonic on G                                      |

Across week 2 you drill lydian (on G and C), mixolydian (on G and D), and aeolian on G (connecting back to week 1's minor material). Ionian on G is implicit from the scales block.

Over the full 2-week G block you've hit all 7 modes on G as same-tonic, plus 4 same-key displacements. The paired days (Mon-Tue, Wed-Thu) reinforce each mode twice, with tonic held constant one day and color held constant the next — two ways of hearing the same thing.

*What you play during the 4 min for each mode:*

1. The mode ascending and descending, 3 octaves, against a drone on the mode's tonic. Slow, with metronome. (~1 min)
1. *Characteristic degree isolation.* Every mode has a note that defines its color — the one that distinguishes it from ionian or aeolian. Play: tonic → characteristic degree → tonic, a few times, listening. Then the day's scaffold cell, a short phrase built to make the characteristic degree audible. (~1 min)

Characteristic degrees and scaffold cells:

|Mode      |Characteristic degree          |Scaffold cell                            |
|----------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
|Dorian    |raised 6 (M6 above tonic)      |1 – 5 – 6 – 5 – b3                       |
|Phrygian  |flat 2 (m2 above tonic)        |1 – b2 – 1, then b2 – b3 – b2            |
|Lydian    |raised 4 (tritone above tonic) |1 – 3 – #4 – 5                           |
|Mixolydian|flat 7 (m7 above tonic)        |1 – b7 – 1, then 5 – b7 – 1              |
|Aeolian   |flat 6 (m6 above tonic)        |1 – b6 – 5, then 4 – b6 – 5              |
|Locrian   |flat 5 (dim 5 above tonic)     |1 – b5 – 1, then b2 – b5                 |

Scaffold cell defaults on for the first two cycles per mode, then retires automatically. You can re-enable it any time.

1. *Tonic triad and characteristic chord, ~1 min.*

Two arpeggios, then alternate between them.

First: arpeggiate the mode's tonic triad, 2 octaves up and down. For G dorian that's G minor (G-Bb-D). ~15 sec.

Then: arpeggiate the characteristic chord, 2 octaves up and down. For G dorian that's C major, the IV chord (C-E-G). ~15 sec.

Then alternate — tonic arp, characteristic arp, tonic arp, characteristic arp — for the remaining ~30 sec. Listen. What makes dorian sound like dorian is the major IV chord coexisting with the minor tonic. That E natural (raised 6) lives inside the C major arpeggio. This is the mode's color heard as a harmonic event, not just a scale degree.

Characteristic chord reference table:

|Mode      |Tonic triad  |Characteristic chord                             |
|----------|-------------|-------------------------------------------------|
|Ionian    |I major      |(none — the reference point)                     |
|Dorian    |i minor      |**IV major**                                     |
|Phrygian  |i minor      |**bII major**                                    |
|Lydian    |I major      |**II major**                                     |
|Mixolydian|I major      |**bVII major**                                   |
|Aeolian   |i minor      |**iv minor** (the b6 heard in the subdominant)   |
|Locrian   |i° diminished|(tonic triad itself is the characteristic event — the tritone 1–b5 is the color; no separate chord)|

1. *Free improvisation in the mode, 1 min.*

Drone still going. No metronome. You're making the mode sound — not running exercises. This step is non-negotiable; it's where theory converts to hearing.

*App display during modal work.* The app shows, for the day's mode:

- Mode name and full note list (e.g., "G Dorian: G A Bb C D E F")
- Characteristic degree (e.g., "E — raised 6 above G")
- Scaffold cell (if enabled for this mode)
- Tonic triad and characteristic chord, with note spellings
- Which step of the 4-step sequence is currently active, with a countdown timer

The display is ambient — not something you stare at, but something you can glance at mid-practice to check. Over time you stop needing it for the modes you've drilled.

*Drone for modal work.* Always on the mode's tonic. For G dorian, drone = G. For C dorian (same-key displacement from G minor), drone = C. This differs from the scales block, where the drone moves through scale degrees. In modal work the tonic stays fixed and the color shifts around it.

**Chord-scale fluency, 3 min.**

The app plays a chord progression as a loop. You improvise over it, matching your scale choice to each chord. When the chord changes, your scale changes. The reflex you're training: *chord information → scale choice → hand position*, without conscious calculation.

*Progression rotation across the week:*

|Day|Type                              |Example in G minor week              |Example in G major week              |
|---|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
|Mon|ii-V-i (functional, core)         |Am7b5 → D7phryg-dom → Gm7            |Am7 → D7 → Gmaj7                     |
|Tue|Extended functional               |Cm7 → Am7b5 → D7phryg-dom → Gm7      |Em7 → Am7 → D7 → Gmaj7               |
|Wed|Modal vamp (first mode)           |Loop Gm7 (play G dorian)             |Loop Gmaj7 (play G ionian)           |
|Thu|Modal vamp (second mode)          |Loop Gm7b9 (play G phrygian)         |Loop Gmaj7#11 (play G lydian)        |
|Fri|Modal vamp (third mode, rotates)  |Loop Gmaj7#11 (play G lydian) — rotates across cycles|Loop G7 (play G mixolydian) — rotates|

Wed/Thu/Fri modal vamps cycle across 24-week loops so you don't always end Friday on the same mode. The app tracks which modes you've drilled on each tonic and rotates accordingly.

*For each chord, the app shows the scale to play.* This is the learning tool. Example for Monday (G minor, Am7b5 → D7 → Gm7):

- Am7b5 → **A locrian** (A Bb C D Eb F G)
- D7 → **D phrygian dominant** (D Eb F# G A Bb C) — the 5th mode of G harmonic minor
- Gm7 → **G dorian** (G A Bb C D E F) or G aeolian

Why these specific scales: each contains every chord tone of the chord it's assigned to, plus useful tensions. Phrygian dominant on D7 in a G minor key = the scale that gives D its altered tensions (b9, b13) straight out of G harmonic minor. Dorian on Gm7 = the "jazz minor" default, brighter than aeolian because of the raised 6.

*The assigned scale is a default for reflex-building, not the only option.* After ~4 weeks on a given progression type, the app offers 2-3 alternate scale choices per chord. You pick what to practice. D7 can be played with altered scale (7th mode of melodic minor), whole-tone, half-whole diminished, or lydian dominant — each produces a different color. Phrygian dominant is the starting default because it's the most idiomatic fit for minor-key V chords.

*What you do during the 3 min:*

1. First 30 sec: listen to the loop, don't play. Hear where the changes fall. Internalize the bar count.
1. Next 2 min: play through the loop. Only use notes from the assigned scale for each chord. No cheating. When the chord changes, switch scales cleanly — even if it means you stop momentarily. The goal is clean scale switches, not continuous phrasing.
1. Final 30 sec: with the reflex warmer, play musical phrases. You'll start stringing lines across chord changes, and the switches will feel less like gear-shifts.

*Bars per chord.* Start at 8 bars per chord — plenty of time to orient. Bump down manually to 4, then 2, then 1 as the reflex develops. Bar count persists per progression type, not per key, because the skill transfers.

*App features needed for this block:*

- **Chord progression library** with the day-of-week defaults above, editable. Each progression stores: chords (with root, quality, extensions), assigned scale per chord (plus alternates once unlocked), bars-per-chord.
- **Chord playback** via Web Audio. Piano-ish synthesized sound for functional progressions, pad/sustained sound for modal vamps. The sound doesn't need to be beautiful — it needs to be *clear* so you hear the chord changes.
- **Visual chord chart** — current chord highlighted as it plays, next chord ghosted. Scale name and notes shown below the current chord.
- **Bars-per-chord control** — toggle between 8 / 4 / 2 / 1. Persists per progression.
- **Tempo** — independent from the scales block metronome, since this is improvisatory. Default 72 bpm or so. Adjustable.
- **Custom progressions** — user can input their own chord sequences and scale assignments. Useful for when you want to drill specific changes for a piece (like the Bach).

*App logic summary for the scales block:*

**State persisted in browser (IndexedDB):**

- Start date — derives current week number and therefore current tonic + mode (minor or major)
- Session log — every completed session: date, week, tonic, minor-or-major, day-of-week, which sub-blocks were completed, tempo used, bowing used, freeform notes field
- Tempo per key — persists across cycles. When a tonic comes back around in 24 weeks, returning tempo defaults to 85% of last cycle's max (adjustable). Avoids both "start fresh at 60" and "pick up at a tempo you haven't touched in 6 months."
- Mode drill history per tonic — which modes have been drilled on G, D, A, etc., and how many times. Previous cycle's session notes visible in a collapsed "last time you were here" panel when a tonic returns.
- Tempo per mode — tracked separately; some modes settle faster than others.
- Modal vamp rotation — which mode was used on Wed/Thu/Fri last time for each tonic, to advance the rotation.
- Adagio/Fuga passage log — which phrase or measure range you worked on which day.

**On session launch:**

Computes week number → tonic + min/maj. Computes day-of-week → drone pitch, bowing, scale form, modal focus, chord-scale progression. Loads current tempo for the key. Loads last session's notes for this key. Starts drone sample looping. Starts metronome.

**Session flow (scales block):**

Each sub-block (Technical, Modal, Chord-scale) has its own [Start] and [Done] buttons. User taps Start when ready; inter-sub-block pauses are allowed and are counted into the session's active-time clock. Each sub-block internally runs its own timed sub-sequence (e.g. 5 steps in Technical) — each step has a countdown and chimes to the next automatically. A step can be extended via [Hold step +30s] if needed.

Block transitions (Scales → Adagio → Fuga → Improv) show a brief summary screen with an [Add notes] link (optional) and an auto-advance after 2 seconds, or manual [Continue] tap.

**"Bump tempo" button:**

Always accessible during the scales block. App suggests a bump (+6 bpm) after 5 clean sessions at current tempo, but waits for your confirm. A "reset tempo" button per key handles the case where last cycle's tempo was too aggressive.

**Session completion:**

At end of session, prompt for a freeform note ("what happened today?"). One to three sentences, saved with the session record. This is the data that actually matters over months.

**Recording:**

A record button is accessible throughout the session, but especially relevant during the improv block. Uses the browser's MediaRecorder API (phone or desktop mic).

Each recording is stored as an audio blob in IndexedDB with metadata: date, session block, key, mode, constraint used (for improv), patch version (for system improv), freeform label.

A recordings library view lets you:

- Browse by date, key, mode, constraint, or patch version
- Play back with timeline markers showing timestamped notes (click to seek)
- Filter by tag (worked / didn't work)
- Export as audio files (.webm or .mp3 depending on browser)

Storage: compress at 64kbps mono (fine for listening back to your own practice). "Delete after 30 days unless starred" option so the library doesn't balloon.

**Platform — browser + phone:**

Build as a Progressive Web App (PWA). Same codebase runs in desktop browser and installed-to-home-screen on iOS and Android. Works offline once assets are cached.

Mobile-specific notes:

- iOS requires user interaction (a tap) before any audio plays — app must start the drone / metronome in response to a button press, not on page load. Standard PWA pattern.
- Microphone access requires explicit permission per session on most browsers. The record button prompts for it.
- Audio latency on phones is generally fine for a drone + metronome + loop playback scenario, though not for real-time effects processing.
- Screen stays awake during session (Wake Lock API) so it doesn't sleep mid-practice.

**Cross-device sync:**

Sessions are local to the device. Manual export/import JSON. No cloud account in MVP.

**Export:**

JSON dump of full session history. Plus audio recordings exportable individually or as a zip.

### Adagio — Piece 1 (6 min)

One passage per day, deep. Intonation, bow distribution, ornament shapes. Don't run the whole movement daily — grooves in what's already there.

*Piece configuration.* Adagio is Piece 1 by default. Configured in settings with: piece name, total measures, measures per chunk (default 4), optional reference drone pitch (e.g. G for Bach G minor Adagio). Current chunk advances automatically when marked mastered — if current is mm. 12–14, next is mm. 14–16 (1-bar overlap so you practice the seam). Chunk size adjustable mid-piece: shrink if a section is dense, widen if it's flowing.

*App logic:*

State tracked:

- Piece metadata: name, total measures, chunk size, reference drone pitch (optional)
- Current chunk — derived from last-mastered position + overlap
- Mastered chunks — running list with dates
- Recordings per session, tied to chunk
- Notes per session, tied to chunk — carry forward and resurface next time you work that chunk

Session flow:

- App shows current chunk (e.g. "mm. 12–14") and the **3 most recent notes you wrote on this chunk**, with a "show all" link for full history on that chunk
- **Drone and metronome default OFF**, both independently togglable on-screen. Reference drone pitch is pre-loaded (G by default for the Adagio) but silent until you enable it.
- Large [Start] button. On tap, 6-min timer begins.
- During the 6 min: you play. Screen is minimal — just the timer, the chunk label, and toggles for drone/metronome/record. You can tap [Record] any time to capture audio, but it's not required during practice.
- When timer ends: prompt sequence:
  1. **"Record a take?"** — offers a quick recording of the current state of the chunk. Skip or record.
  2. **Notes prompt** — freeform text, tagged worked / didn't work / neutral. Required (at least one note).
  3. **"Have you mastered this passage?"** — Yes / Not yet. If Yes: chunk advances (with 1-bar overlap) and current goes to mastered list.

Weekly review (Fridays):

- Runthrough of chunks mastered in the last 8 weeks + 3 random from the older archive
- Full archive runthrough user-triggered, monthly
- Mark any that's slipped as "needs refresh" — returns to active rotation

### Fuga — Piece 2 (12 min)

The Fuga is your chord etude. Every Fuga passage has both the physical roll problem and the voice-leading problem. You work whichever one the current chunk demands.

*Piece configuration.* Fuga is Piece 2 by default. Same setup as Piece 1: piece name, total measures, chunk size (default 6), optional reference drone. Both pieces are re-configurable — if you switch to a different pair later, update in settings.

**Two practices available within any session, applied as the passage requires:**

*Roll drill.* The physical reality: you can't sound four strings simultaneously with a curved bridge. What you can do is roll them. Break the chord 2+2 — bottom pair first, top pair arrives. Some chords want 1+3 or 3+1 depending on where the melodic voice lives.

The drill: take one chord. Play the bottom two strings alone, sustained, even weight, clean intonation. Then the top two alone, same. Then the full chord, breaking 2+2, *slowly* — 3-4 seconds per chord. Feel the bow weight transfer from lower strings to upper. The bottom pair should decay as the top pair enters, but not disappear — they're still sounding, just quieter. You're practicing the *roll itself* as a musical gesture.

Then move to chord *pairs* — two adjacent chords in the passage. Roll the first, roll the second, listen to whether the voices actually connect. Usually they don't, and you find out which finger is lifting too early or landing in the wrong place.

*Voice isolation.* Fuga is 3 or 4 voices depending on where you are. Play each voice as its own melodic line. Soprano alone, top to bottom. Then alto. Then tenor. Then bass.

Then pairs: soprano + alto, alto + tenor. This is where you discover that the fingering you've been using kills the inner voice.

Goal: be able to sing any voice from memory after a week on a passage. If you can't sing it, you're not hearing it, which means the listener isn't either.

Fingerings are architecture. Compare Szeryng, Milstein, Enescu editions — pick fingerings where the hand doesn't reshape mid-chord. Steal.

*App logic:*

State tracked:

- Piece metadata (same as Piece 1)
- Current chunk and voice count (2, 3, or 4)
- Mastered chunks — running list with dates
- Recordings and notes per session, tied to chunk — notes carry forward and resurface on that chunk next session

Session flow:

- Shows current chunk, voice count, and **3 most recent notes on this chunk** (show-all available)
- Drone and metronome default OFF, both independently togglable
- Large [Start] button. On tap, 12-min timer begins.
- No rigid sub-sequence. You work the chunk however it needs — roll the chords, isolate voices, combine.
- [Record] available throughout
- When timer ends: same prompt sequence as Adagio:
  1. "Record a take?"
  2. Notes prompt (required)
  3. "Have you mastered this passage?" — Yes advances chunk with overlap, Not yet continues

Weekly review (Fridays, alongside Adagio runthrough):

- Runthrough of chunks mastered in the last 8 weeks + 3 random older
- Monthly full-archive runthrough user-triggered
- Mark slipped passages for refresh

### Improv (15 min)

Two distinct practices, balanced across the week: **solo acoustic improv** and **system improv** (within the fixed electronic setup). Different skills, different problems, don't conflate.

**Why two.** Acoustic improv trains your relationship with the instrument — bow, body, silence, the physical material. System improv trains your relationship with the patch — its latencies, its responses, where it pushes back, where it disappears. The system has to become transparent before it stops being a distraction, and that only happens through sustained engagement with *the same patch*, not by rotating constraints each day.

*Weekly split:*

|Day|Mode                 |Why                                                                         |
|---|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Mon|System               |Start the week learning the patch                                           |
|Tue|System               |Same patch, no changes — you're getting to know it                          |
|Wed|System               |Three consecutive sessions in the same configuration builds real familiarity|
|Thu|Acoustic             |Reset the ear, no electronics                                               |
|Fri|System               |Fourth day on the patch — should feel transparent                           |
|Sun|Long session (30 min)|Alternates weekly: system one week, acoustic the next                       |

Four system days clustered (Mon-Wed + Fri) so the patch can stay fixed across a stretch. Rowe's point about the instrument including the table — you're not designing a new setup each day, you're studying the one you have until its behavior is predictable.

*Ambient reminder at top of improv screen:* "This week: G, [today's mode] today." The week's tonic and the day's mode from the modal block are shown as available material, not prescribed. Use them or don't.

-----

**System improv — how to practice it.**

State the patch explicitly before the session: delay time, feedback, any filters or modulations, what's feeding what. The patch is the constraint. The goal is not to design it but to learn its behavior.

Practice inside the patch with secondary constraints layered on:

- One pitch, explore what the system does with it across 10+ min (Lucier-style — the delay line makes every small variation compound)
- Rhythmic feeding — only articulate on downbeats, or only on offbeats, or only in bursts
- Dynamic-only variation — same material, vary only amplitude
- Register-only — same rhythmic/dynamic material, vary only pitch register
- Modulate slowly — a semitone drift across 2 min, hear how the delay line fights or reinforces

Secondary constraint rotates across the week so you're not doing the same thing four days running.

Patch changes every 1–2 weeks, not daily. When you do change the patch, spend a session just listening to what it does, no plan — a calibration session before using it for anything.

*Patch versioning.* Every edit to the patch description creates a versioned record with date and full text. Every recording stamps to the active patch version. Later you can filter: "show me all recordings from patch v3."

-----

**Acoustic improv — how to practice it.**

No electronics. Naked violin. Constraint rotates per session:

- One pitch only, 15 min, Lucier-style — let small changes become events
- Only sul pont / only noise, no pitched material
- Left hand only, no bow (pizz, tapping, hammer-ons)
- Modal work in the week's mode — feeds the Bach when G minor comes around
- Single bow length — one down-bow stretched to 30 sec, then one up-bow, no tempo
- Silence-first — start silent, add sound only when it feels inevitable

Thursday is the acoustic day in the standard week. Sunday's long session alternates acoustic/system across weeks.

-----

**Sunday long session (30 min).**

One constraint held for the full duration. Material runs out around minute 15 — what happens after that is the actual training. Minute 22 is where you find something you didn't know you had. Alternates system (one week) / acoustic (the next). This replaces a standard practice day — Sunday isn't in addition to the 5x weekday cycle, it's a separate thing.

-----

*App logic for improv:*

State tracked:

- Current patch (for system days) — described in text, versioned. Each edit creates a new version.
- Acoustic constraint library, with rotation tracker
- Every session recorded with metadata: date, mode (system/acoustic), patch version or constraint, duration, feeling score (1-5), focus score (1-5)
- Listen-back queue for recordings not yet annotated
- Per recording: timestamped notes with "worked" / "didn't work" tags

Session flow (system or acoustic):

- App shows: current patch description + version (system) OR today's constraint (acoustic)
- For system: "Session N of this patch — is it feeling transparent yet?"
- Secondary constraint for today (rotated) for system days
- [Setup ready? Start] button. Timer is paused until tapped — gives you time to reconfigure hardware, tune, breathe.
- On Start: 15-min timer + recording begin simultaneously
- During the session: optional [Note] button to drop timestamped notes mid-play (not required in-session)
- When timer ends: recording stops automatically. Prompt sequence:
  1. **Feeling gauge:** 1–5 slider "how did that feel"
  2. **Focus gauge:** 1–5 slider "how focused were you"
  3. **Notes prompt:** freeform text, tagged worked / didn't work (at least one required before advancing — but you can add more later)
  4. **"Listen back now, or later?"**
     - If now: waveform timeline appears, tap points to drop more timestamped notes with worked/didn't-work tags. Save and close.
     - If later: recording goes into listen-back queue. Banner will surface next day until annotated.

Sunday long session flow:

- Same as above, but 30-min timer
- Note prompt template: "what emerged? what was the shape? what happened at minute 22?"
- Requires 3+ annotations (either during, after, or via listen-back) before next improv block unlocks

Patch change prompt:

- After 5+ sessions on a single patch, app asks weekly: "Keep this patch, or change?"
- When you change, app archives the old patch description with its session count and recordings, then asks you to describe the new one

Listen-back enforcement (improv-scoped only):

- Tomorrow's improv block can't start until yesterday's improv recording has 2+ timestamped notes (Sunday long session: 3+)
- Scales, Adagio, Fuga run regardless — the gate only pressures the block where the habit matters
- A banner on the Home screen surfaces any unannotated recording

-----

### Session close

After all blocks complete:

- **Streak display** — current streak (consecutive weeks with 5+ sessions), best streak, weeks-at-current-streak
- **Active session time** — wall-clock elapsed from first Start tap to Save (includes inter-block breaks)
- Blocks completed checklist
- Recordings captured with durations
- Notes captured count
- **"How did today feel?"** — 1–5 slider
- [Save and close]

On save: session marked complete, week counter advances if 5th session of the week, tomorrow's key/mode/drone previewed.

### Light day mode

Toggle available at session start. For fatigue, injury, low-energy days.

- Halves each block's time (22-23 min total session)
- Skips tempo-bump prompts
- Skips listen-back gate for tomorrow's improv
- Session still counts toward weekly total, flagged as "light"

Keeps the practice alive on bad days without grooving in either skipping-entirely or pushing-through-injury.

## Timeline — honest

"True mastery" isn't a finish line. Here's what's real:

- **Drone intonation.** Noticeable shift in 6–8 weeks of daily drone work. Ear opens up. Solid in 6 months. Deeply internalized across years — this is a lifelong calibration.
- **Modal fluency.** 3 months of consistent modal work and you'll hear/produce any mode from any tonic without thinking. Applying it fluently in improv takes longer — 6–12 months.
- **Chord-scale reflex.** 2–3 months before the fingers go where the changes say, without effort. Alternate scale choices and non-functional harmony fluency are open-ended.
- **Fuga chord technique.** The Fuga itself takes 6–12 months to play with real voice independence. The underlying chord sensibility grows across decades.
- **Improv chops.** No endpoint. But: the constraint-work + recording + listening loop produces visible growth in 6–8 weeks. A year in, your vocabulary is a different thing. The long-form stamina piece — holding 30+ min with material — takes a year of regular long sessions to feel natural.

For the show in a few weeks: drone intonation and scale-to-improv translation will already show real gains. Chord fluency in the Fuga won't — that's a long game. Don't measure against it.

-----

## Principles underneath all of this

- Scales do three jobs, not one. Intonation, geography, theory — practice them as three jobs.
- Chords are distributed across time. You're not sounding them, you're voicing them.
- Record improv. Listen next day. Without this you groove in habits.
- Know your system cold. The delay, the patch, the setup — spend sessions where nothing changes so the system becomes transparent.
- Form is trainable. Long-form stamina is a separate skill from short-form invention.
- Listening is practice. 10 min tracking one sound in a room is ear training for the actual material of the work.

## Open questions for implementation

Items that need resolution before or during the build.

### Audio

- **Drone samples.** License-cleared tanpura/shruti samples (some free, some paid) vs. synthesis. Synthesis is simpler but less musically satisfying — the drone has to be *pleasant to sit with* for 12 minutes. Recommend: license a good tanpura set, pre-render per tonic.
- **Drone pitch rotation.** Pre-rendered per pitch (12 samples per drone) rather than real-time shift — keeps JI temperament clean, avoids iOS Safari artifacts.
- **Temperament.** Default JI for drones. ET as a setting. JI beats lock decisively, which is the point. Pre-rendering per tonic means each sample is already in JI for its tonic — no shifting math needed.
- **Reference pitch.** Default A=440, configurable (for anyone playing with ensembles at A=442, A=415, etc.).
- **Chord playback for chord-scale fluency.** Synthesized piano for functional progressions, sustained pad for modal vamps. Doesn't need to be beautiful — needs to be clear.
- **Metronome.** Tone.js handles scheduling properly. Don't ship on setInterval.

### Recording

- **Format.** MediaRecorder defaults: Chrome/Android = webm/opus, iOS Safari = mp4/aac. Normalize on export or accept the mix.
- **Bitrate.** 64kbps mono (~4.8MB per 10 min). 128kbps if clarity matters for self-critique.
- **Storage lifecycle.** Auto-delete recordings >30 days old unless starred. Surface in settings.
- **Timestamped notes UX.** Audio pauses on note-tap, resumes on save. Notes editable/deletable. Exportable as sidecar JSON.
- **Sample rate.** 44.1kHz mono.
- **Recordings library.** Filter by date, key, mode, constraint, patch version, tags. Timeline markers click-to-seek.

### Platform / PWA

- **Tech stack.** React + Vite + IndexedDB via Dexie + Tone.js for audio.
- **iOS PWA limitations.** No background audio. Push notifications limited. Wake Lock works but can be revoked.
- **Notifications.** Email or SMS more reliable than web push on iOS, or in-app banner on next open.
- **Offline mode.** Full offline after first load. Service worker for asset caching.

### Data model

- **User accounts.** MVP: local-only. Include user-id field (UUID) from day one for later cloud sync.
- **Cross-device sync.** Not in MVP. Later: Supabase/Firebase, last-write-wins.
- **Export format.** JSON for metadata. ZIP containing audio files keyed to session IDs.
- **Import.** Reverse of export. Validate schema version.

### Onboarding

First-run flow, target under 5 minutes:

1. Welcome + philosophy (one screen, skippable)
1. Set start date (defaults to today)
1. Reference pitch (default A=440) and temperament (default JI)
1. Handedness (default right)
1. **Drone sound preset** — choose from bank (tanpura warm, tanpura bright, shruti, sine+saw, etc. — Tunable-style variety)
1. **Metronome sound preset** — choose from bank (wood block, cowbell, clave, electronic beep, etc.)
1. **Piece 1** — name (default "Adagio"), total measures, chunk size (default 4), reference drone pitch (optional). Can skip.
1. **Piece 2** — name (default "Fuga"), total measures, chunk size (default 6), reference drone pitch (optional). Can skip.
1. Describe current system patch (free text). Can skip if starting acoustic-only.
1. Done. Land on today's session.

All "skip" paths let the user add items from settings later.

### Session state edge cases

- **Missed weeks.** Week counter advances by *sessions completed*, not calendar time. Skip a week, resume where you were.
- **Partial sessions.** Count toward 5-per-week with a note that specific blocks were skipped. Improv listen-back gate still applies.
- **Pause mode.** Explicit "pause cycle" button that freezes the week counter until un-paused.
- **Tempo regression across cycles.** Returning to a tonic defaults to 85% of last cycle's max. "Reset tempo" button per key for full restart.
- **Light day.** Counts as a session. Flagged as "light" in the session log.

### Visual design

**Aesthetic principle.** Swiss / Dieter Rams. Flat, functional, information-first. No skeuomorphism, no gradients, no drop shadows, no card/tile contrast. Vector shapes. Sans-serif typography (Inter or Helvetica-family). Generous whitespace. Unornamented.

**Color.** Each block displays a single bold solid color as its ground, randomly chosen from the palette at block transition (so the color changes as the session progresses — signals the block change visually). Palette: saturated blues, greens, oranges, reds, browns, yellows, purples. No pastels. Text and UI elements sit directly on the colored ground in one contrasting ink color (off-white or near-black depending on ground luminosity). No tiles, no windows, no floating cards — shapes and type live on the ground directly.

**Layout principle.** One persistent screen per block. Top band: block name + timer. Middle: current content (scale notation, chunk label, constraint). Bottom band: controls. Sub-blocks transition within the same screen, not via navigation. Drawer pulled up from the bottom when needed for drone/metronome/volume controls.

**Musical notation.** Rendered inline via VexFlow (SVG output). Used in the scales block for every sub-step: scale in the current mode/form, broken thirds, arpeggios. Dynamic per key. Note-name text still shown beneath for reference.

**Large tap targets.** Minimum 60px. User is holding a violin. Note-tap area is the entire screen body for mid-play note-dropping.

**Dark mode.** System-default-aware. Bold colors remain bold; ink color inverts.

**Gamification (light touch).**
- Streak counter on Home screen and at session close (consecutive weeks with 5+ sessions; pause mode doesn't break it)
- Completion animations per sub-block — a simple vector checkmark or bar-fill, ~400ms, not intrusive
- Session-close summary shows streak status, feeling score trend (last 7 sessions)
- No XP, no levels, no badges — this is a practice tool, not a game

**Independent volume controls.**
- Drone volume (with toggle)
- Metronome volume (with toggle)
- App system volume (ducks all app audio together, separate from OS volume)
- All three adjustable during session via pull-up drawer

**Sound variety (Tunable-inspired).**
- Drone bank: multiple tanpura variants, shruti, synth pad options, sine-based minimalist — user picks default in settings, overridable per session
- Metronome bank: wood block, cowbell, clave, electronic beep, side-stick, etc. — user picks default
- Settings for both in one place, previewable before committing

## Decisions locked
